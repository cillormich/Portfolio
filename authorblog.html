BEST WAYS to follow/handle people who often front themselves as "more intelligent and hardworking."


  1. Never be jealous or envious of them. Never! Your brain is wired in a different way. You just have to understand what works best for you.

  2. In any discussion or argument with them, always leave them with these words: "You're right." Agree with whatever they're saying but what you'll do is registered in your mind.

  3. There's no competition in being intelligent and no one knows it all. You must protect your sanity at all times by "knowing" and "working" the best you can, you must not know and do everything. Be flexible, open-minded and happy.

  4. Use them instead. Use their knowledge, allow them to do most of the work, and let the stress of labour choke them while you continue to clear your head and your own space.

  5. As long as you get your job done, you have nothing to worry about. Always limit your efforts to your job description. Life is too short for you to be giving yourself some additional stress.

  6. Embrace social intelligence instead. Have a pleasant personality and learn the best way to socialise and relate with people. It is much more comforting.

  7. Finally, you must get better in the art of manoeuvre. Rather than isolate yourself from the power games people play, learn the best possible moves you can make to guarantee your safety.

Life & Struggle

Every day we wake up, we are grateful to the Creator for seeing the newness of the day and for being alive. Although most of us don't pray but rather go straight to our phone, God is so good and merciful that He doesn't count that for us. If we're in love, next we think about our partner, then our family and our job or hustle. Most of us come back home tired and broken, just keeping our heads above water and waiting for the month-end salary for us to smile again, go shopping and take care of our needs. And like a flash, the money is finished and we are back toiling and hoping again and again. On the other side of the struggling spectrum are those who live by the daily grind, if they fail to leave their house any day they won't find what to eat and if they have a family, there will be crisis at home. The truth of the matter is that whether it's the monthly paying job or the daily hustle, money is never enough. Unfortunately, while most of us make ends meet by the sweat of our brows, others make millions and billions by the push of a button---the very scarce and insatiable money encircling among the rich. While no one should blame or curse anyone for being rich, life, regardless, is unarguably unfair and is never balanced---what we get in quantum, others get in abundance and even more. And as crazy as it seems, those who need money most are those who don't get it, and their number is like the sands of the earth. Be that as it may, what challenges most of us every day and limits our progress is a general lack of understanding of the world we live in and the laws that govern the universe. Only when we know that we can be able to navigate life no matter its challenges, and to know what truly matters and what doesn't. This is for all those who struggle every day for survival. NEVER GIVE UP!
